    The problem

    Great software, built in the wrong postcode.

    Application declined

    "Unfortunately, Stripe is not currently available in your country."

    The message thousands of African founders know by heart. Subscription billing — the engine of every SaaS business — is gated behind payment processors that won't onboard them, freeze funds without warning, or settle in days while taking 3% off the top.

    SubSol removes the gatekeeper. Subscriptions live directly on Solana as on-chain accounts. A merchant needs a wallet — not an approval letter. A customer approves once, and USDC moves on schedule for as long as the subscription runs.

    Settlement is measured in seconds, not banking days. The protocol takes a flat 0.5% — and nothing else.

    How it works

    Three instructions. The whole machine.

    1

    Customer approves once

    An SPL token delegate grants the subscription authority to move exactly the agreed amount — create_subscription writes the terms to a PDA. SubSol never takes custody of anyone's funds.

    2

    The schedule lives on-chain

    Amount, interval, merchant, next-payment time — all stored in the subscription account. There is no server to keep alive, no webhook to babysit, no database to lose.

    3

    Every cycle, payment fires

    process_payment checks the clock, moves USDC to the merchant, takes the 0.5% fee, and sets the next due date. The customer can walk away anytime with cancel_subscription.
